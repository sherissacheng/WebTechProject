Favorite Movies

Fallen Angels
by Wong Kar-wai

Fallen Angels is the second movie of Wong Kar-wai's I've watched and definitely sits near the top of my list of movies I've enjoyed. I particularly liked He Zhiwu: though mute, his actions are hilarious yet thoughtful, and he shows a lot of personality throughout the movie. I love how Cantonese wasn't the only spoken language; the Mandarin provided by He Zhiwu kept the dialogue fresh and interesting. I enjoyed seeing each character's position in life, though I did feel that they were hardly connected until the end.

Howl's Moving Castle
by Hayao Miyazaki

Howl's Moving Castle is the first and only Studio Ghibli movie I've seen so far (which is truly a crime, I definitely need to watch more of them!). I love Miyazaki's world-building: every character is so unique in both personality and physical characteristics. The movie felt like a dream - I couldn't have predicted what Miyazaki would do next. Sophie and Howl are so sweet, and I love Calcifer!

Dead Poets Society
by Peter Weir

Dead Poets Society is a Dark Academia dream: the romanticisation of school, the secret meetings to shout poetry and inspire others with words, and the honors society. Like the name suggests, there is a dark side to all of this, and Dead Poets Society is no different. This movie left me with a lot of inspiring quotes, and I particularly loved Neil.

Favorite TV Shows

Merlin
by Julie Gardner

For a TV show that was produced in 2008, I think the quality isn't too bad. I'm sure I would cringe looking back at it now, but for middle-school me this show was hilarious and magical. The fact that I still remember how this show ended speaks to my dissatisfaction with it though. I miss Arthur and Merlin's banter.

Queen's Gambit
by Scott Frank

As someone who was in the chess club in elementary school, and have since casually played, Queen's Gambit was very fun watch. I adore Beth's awkwardness around people and how it all disappears the moment she sits in front of a chess board. Her ambition is admirable, and I found it really interesting how Netflix tied in drug addiction with chess.

Attack on Titan
by Hajime Isayama

I feel like Attack on Titan is a given for anyone who watches anime. The plot is so beautifully written and connected, and there were so many plot twists that I didn't expect at all in the moment, but looking back was able to find the little hints everywhere. Every character has so much development - Attack on Titan is truly a work of art and is definitely one of my favorite shows of all time.

Haikyu!!
by Haruichi Furadate

Haikyu!! was the first ever anime I watched, so it definitely holds a special place in my heart. Hinata and Kageyama's dynamic is so fun, and while there are intense moments in the show, it feels lighthearted as whole. Whenever I feel nostalgic, I pull up Haikyu!! My favorite character is Akaashi, he's such a calm character and he balances out Bokuto's energy.
